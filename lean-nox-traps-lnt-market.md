<p><p>Lean NOx Traps (LNT) are advanced emission control technologies primarily used in diesel engines to reduce nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions. They operate on a principle that captures NOx from exhaust gases under lean air-fuel conditions, storing it until a richer combustion phase triggers its reduction to nitrogen and water vapor. This makes LNTs integral to meeting stringent environmental regulations and standards in the automotive and industrial sectors.</p><p>The Lean NOx Traps (LNT) Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing regulatory pressures for cleaner emissions and a rising demand for fuel-efficient vehicles. As manufacturers look to improve the efficiency of internal combustion engines while managing emissions, LNTs are becoming a popular choice.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in material science and trap design are enhancing the effectiveness and reliability of LNT systems, further propelling market expansion. <p><p>Lean NOx Traps (LNT) are emission control devices used in vehicles to reduce nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions from diesel engines. Active LNTs utilize additional systems like fuel injection or a secondary fuel source to periodically regenerate the catalyst material, ensuring optimal performance under various conditions. Passive LNTs, on the other hand, rely on the engine's normal operating conditions to achieve regeneration, making them more straightforward but less adaptable. Both types cater to different market needs based on vehicle applications and emissions regulations.</p></p>
