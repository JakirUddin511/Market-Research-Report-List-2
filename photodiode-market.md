<p><p>The photodiode market is primarily segmented into silicon photodiodes and InGaAs photodiodes. Silicon photodiodes are widely used for visible light detection due to their cost-effectiveness and efficiency in the 400-1100 nm wavelength range. In contrast, InGaAs photodiodes excel in near-infrared applications, operating effectively in the 800-1700 nm range. These sensors are essential in telecommunications, spectroscopy, and industrial applications. Both types witness increased demand due to advancements in technology and the growing need for efficient light detection solutions.</p></p>
